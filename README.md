# Zomato_Analytics_Dashboard
Analyzed data from Zomato to uncover insights on restaurant ratings, pricing, cuisines, and customer trends. Cleaned datasets using SQL and Excel, and built interactive dashboards in Power BI/Tableau to identify top performers, demand patterns, and growth opportunities.

# Objectives

• Analyze data from Zomato to identify customer ordering trends and preferences.

• Evaluate restaurant performance based on ratings, pricing, and popularity.

• Identify top cuisines, high-demand locations, and peak order times.

• Understand factors influencing customer satisfaction and ratings.

• Generate data-driven insights to support business and marketing decisions.

# Key insights

• Platform spans 141 cities with 9,551 restaurants and 1,826 cuisines, showing wide market coverage.

• Majority of restaurants fall in the ₹0–500 price range, indicating strong presence of budget-friendly options.

• Most restaurants are rated 3–4 stars, suggesting average-to-good service quality dominates.

• North Indian cuisine leads by a large margin, making it the most popular category.

• Restaurant openings show steady growth, peaking around 2018, reflecting platform expansion.

• Only about 12% offer table booking, indicating low adoption of reservation services.

• Around 74% provide online delivery, highlighting delivery as the primary business model.

• New Delhi has the highest restaurant concentration among cities.

# Conclusion
The analysis of Zomato data reveals strong platform growth, wide cuisine diversity, and a dominant presence of budget-friendly restaurants. Most listings maintain moderate ratings, indicating consistent service quality. High adoption of online delivery highlights changing customer preferences, while low table-booking usage suggests an opportunity for feature expansion. Overall, the insights support data-driven strategies for market expansion, customer targeting, and service optimization.
